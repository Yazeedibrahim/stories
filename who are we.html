Who are we


Long ago, in a lush village at the edge of a great forest, lived a wise elder named Mosi. His hair was silver, and his eyes shone with the knowledge of countless generations. He often sat beneath the ancient baobab tree that stood in the village square, its massive branches spreading wide like arms ready to embrace the sky.
To the villagers, this tree was sacred — it was said to be the home of their ancestors' spirits. One evening, as the sun dipped below the horizon, a young boy named Kofi approached Mosi. His heart was heavy with questions, and he sought the elder's wisdom.
"Grandfather Mosi," Kofi said, his voice quivering with curiosity, "How can we truly know the spirits of our ancestors? What did they want from us?" Mosi smiled gently, his old hands resting on his walking stick.
"The spirits of our ancestors live not just in the wind or the earth, but in every one of us," he said. "They are our blood, our history, and our way of life.
They have left us teachings and stories that guide us. To understand them, you must first listen—to the earth, to the voices of the elders, and to the baobab tree." That night, after the moon had risen, Kofi sat beneath the baobab, hoping to hear the wisdom of the ancestors.
As he sat in stillness, he began to feel a warm breeze, carrying with it the scent of sweet grass and earth. The tree's gnarled roots seemed to pulse with life, and slowly, a voice spoke in his mind. *"We are the strength of the lion, the patience of the river, the resilience of the baobab,"* the voice whispered. "We are not gone, Kofi. We live in your every thought, every step, and every decision you make." Kofi closed his eyes, feeling the weight of the message.
As he sat in deep reflection, images of his forefathers flashed before him — warriors, healers, farmers, and storytellers, all working together to build the village and pass on their wisdom. He saw them face hardships, yet they never faltered, their spirits guiding the way. The next morning, Kofi rushed to Mosi. "I heard them! I heard the ancestors in the wind!" he exclaimed. Mosi nodded, his eyes twinkling with pride.
"You have begun to understand, young one. Our ancestors live on in us, and they guide us in everything we do. Remember their wisdom, and carry it with honor. You are not just Kofi; you are the child of those who came before you. Their strength is yours to inherit." From that day on, Kofi became a keeper of the stories and teachings of his ancestors. He shared their wisdom with the village, ensuring that their legacy lived on in the hearts of the people. And whenever he needed guidance, he would return to the baobab tree, where the spirits of the past whispered the lessons of life, reminding him that the strength of his ancestors would always be with him.
